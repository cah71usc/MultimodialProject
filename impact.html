Impact on the Ecosystem

Forest fires drastically alter ecosystems. While some ecosystems have adapted to fire, human-caused fires often happen too frequently or with too much intensity, disrupting natural cycles.

Wildlife loses its habitat, soil becomes less fertile, and water sources can be contaminated with ash and debris. The biodiversity in these areas can suffer tremendously.

Recovery can take years or even decades, and some ecosystems never fully return to their original state.
